Normal vs. Abnormal — When to worry?

It is completely normal to lose 50–100 strands of hair a day. Hair goes through a natural cycle of growth, rest, and shedding.

Normal: A few strands on your brush or in the shower drain.

Abnormal: Clumps of hair, ponytail getting noticeably thinner, or your scalp becoming more visible through your hair (widening partition).

3 MYTHS BUSTED
"ये 3 myths पर believe करते रहे, तो hair fall कभी नहीं रुकेगा."

3 myths keeping your hair fall from actually improving.

✕
Myth 1: "Hair oil stops hair fall."
✓
Oil is a great conditioner for the hair shaft, but it cannot stop hair from falling out of the root. In fact, leaving oil on an oily or dandruff-prone scalp can increase hair fall by blocking follicles.
✕
Myth 2: "Changing your shampoo will fix it."
✓
Shampoo's only job is to clean the scalp. It cannot treat hair fall — it only removes hair that has already detached. A clean scalp is essential, but it alone won't fix the root cause.
✕
Myth 3: "Anti-hair fall shampoos actually work."
✓
Shampoo stays on your scalp for ~30 seconds. It doesn't have enough contact time to penetrate the root and change your hair biology. Real results come from inside.

The real truth 🔬

Hair fall usually starts from the inside. Until the internal cause is identified and treated, no oil, shampoo, or serum will give long-term results.

THE 4 REAL ROOT CAUSES
🩸

Nutritional Deficiency

Low Iron (Ferritin), Vitamin B12, and Vitamin D3 — the #1 cause in India.

⚗️

Hormonal Imbalance

PCOS, thyroid issues, or high DHT shrink hair follicles over time.

🧠

Physical / Mental Stress

High fever, illness, surgery, or extreme stress can "shock" hair into shedding 2–3 months later.

🦠

Scalp Health

Dandruff or seborrheic dermatitis creates an unhealthy environment for hair roots.

Why this matters 💡

Until you identify which of these is affecting you, no external product will create lasting results. Think of it as treating the soil, not the leaves.

IDENTIFY YOUR TYPE
TYPE 1

Sudden Heavy Shedding (Telogen Effluvium)

Hair everywhere — on floor, pillow, clothes. Usually caused by recent stress, illness, or crash dieting. Typically reverses once the trigger is addressed.

TYPE 2

Gradual Thinning (Pattern Hair Loss)

You don't see much "fall," but volume decreases over years. Partition looks wider, or hairline is slowly receding. Needs medical treatment — doesn't self-correct.

THE CORRECT APPROACH
1

Blood Tests First

Check Iron/Ferritin, Vitamin D, B12, and Thyroid levels before spending on products.

2

Fix Scalp Hygiene

Use a shampoo suited to your scalp type (oily vs dry). Keep the "soil" clean and balanced.

3

Treat the Root Cause

Nutritional deficiency → supplement. Hormonal → see a dermatologist. Stress → manage triggers.

4

Medical Topicals (if prescribed)

For pattern thinning, prescribed lotions can improve blood flow to roots. Not OTC guesswork.

YOUR BEST SUPPLEMENT — FOOD

Hair is 90% protein. 🌿

If you don't eat enough protein, your body stops sending it to your hair first. No supplement replaces consistent daily nutrition.

🥚

Protein

Eggs, paneer, dal, soya, sprouts — every meal, every day.

🥬

Iron (Ferritin)

Leafy greens, beetroot, dates, jaggery. Pair with Vitamin C for better absorption.

🌰

Biotin & Zinc

Walnuts, almonds, pumpkin seeds — natural biotin that actually gets absorbed.

☀️

Vitamin D

15 min of morning sunlight + eggs + fortified foods. Deficiency is epidemic in India.

SCALP CARE vs. HAIR CARE
🌱
Scalp Care = Health
Like soil for a plant. Where new hair grows. Cleansing and balance here = long-term results.
✨
Hair Care = Cosmetics
Conditioners and masks treat dead hair. They improve appearance, but don't grow new hair.

Most people over-invest in cosmetic hair care and under-invest in scalp health. Flip that equation.

SEE A DERMATOLOGIST IF
⚠️ Consult Now

Do not delay treatment if:

  • You lose more than 100 hairs a day for more than 4 weeks
  • You notice bald patches (circular spots)
  • Your scalp is itchy, red, or very flaky
  • Hair fall is accompanied by irregular periods or sudden weight gain
  • Your partition is visibly widening over months
🌟 ROOT CAUSE CHECKLIST

Run through this to identify your trigger:

Had a high fever or major illness in the last 3 months?
Daily protein intake less than 0.8g per kg of body weight?
Sleeping less than 6 hours a night?
Living in a hard water area (white stains on buckets)?
Scalp oily again just 24 hours after washing?
Visible white flakes or crust on scalp?
Irregular periods or recent thyroid diagnosis?

If you checked 3 or more — you have identifiable triggers. The next step is a proper diagnosis, not more products.
