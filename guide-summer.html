YOUR SUMMER SKIN GUIDE

Eat Your Way to a Summer Glow.

5 foods to start from tomorrow — for hydrated, healthy, glowing skin this season.

Dr. Pallavi Ahire-Shelke · MBBS, MD, DNB, DDV · Gold Medalist Dermatologist · Baner, Pune

Inner & Outer Beauty · Wholesome Beauty

· · ·

What you eat this summer shows on your skin 🌿

Summer puts your skin under real stress — heat, sweat, sun exposure, and dehydration all work against your natural glow. Most people respond by adding more products. But the most effective changes often start from inside.

These 5 foods are simple, seasonal, and widely available across India. Each one has a specific role in keeping your skin hydrated, protected, and glowing through the hottest months of the year.

"Your skin reflects your plate. In summer especially, what you eat determines how you glow."
— Dr. Pallavi Ahire-Shelke
START FROM TOMORROW
01

🍉 Watermelon

Why It Works

Watermelon is approximately 90% water — making it one of the most effective hydrating foods you can eat in summer. It keeps skin cells plump, reduces dryness, and helps your skin look fresh and dewy through the heat.

How to Have It

One bowl as a mid-morning snack. Eat it fresh and chilled — avoid adding salt, which can counteract the hydration benefit.

Deep skin hydration
02

🥒 Cucumber

Why It Works

Cucumber has a natural cooling effect on the body and contains silica — a mineral that supports skin elasticity. It also helps reduce puffiness and keeps the skin calm during hot, humid days.

How to Have It

Eat 1 cucumber daily — in a salad, with a squeeze of lemon and a pinch of salt, or simply sliced as a snack between meals.

Cooling + de-puffing
03

🥭 Mango

Why It Works

Mango is rich in Vitamin A and antioxidants — both essential for skin cell repair and renewal. Vitamin A supports the skin's natural turnover process, and the antioxidants help fight free radical damage from sun exposure.

How to Have It

One medium mango daily — eaten fresh or blended into a smoothie. Stick to one serving; mango is naturally high in sugar and more is not more when it comes to skin benefit.

Skin repair + glow
04

🥥 Coconut Water

Why It Works

Summer sweating depletes your body of electrolytes — and dehydrated skin looks dull, feels tight, and is more prone to irritation. Coconut water replenishes these electrolytes naturally, keeping skin hydrated from the inside without added sugar.

How to Have It

One glass daily, preferably in the morning before stepping out. Choose fresh coconut water over packaged options wherever possible.

Electrolyte hydration
05

🍅 Tomato

Why It Works

Tomato contains lycopene — a powerful antioxidant that has been shown to provide internal protection against UV-related skin damage. It doesn't replace sunscreen, but it adds a meaningful layer of support from within.

How to Have It

Add one tomato to your daily salad, or have a glass of fresh tomato juice. Cooked tomatoes actually release more lycopene — so sabzi and soups count too.

Sun damage protection
"Food is not a substitute for sunscreen. But it makes everything work better."

These foods support your skin from the inside — but they work best alongside a proper summer skincare routine. SPF is non-negotiable. Diet amplifies it.

COMMON SUMMER MISTAKES
✕

Skipping water for juices

Packaged fruit juices are high in sugar and low in actual hydration benefit for skin. Fresh fruit and plain water are always better than bottled juice.

✕

Eating mango in excess

Mango is excellent for skin in the right quantity. Eating 3–4 mangoes daily spikes blood sugar, which can trigger acne and inflammation — especially in acne-prone skin.

✕

Relying on food alone for sun protection

Lycopene and antioxidants from food offer internal support — but they do not replace broad-spectrum SPF 30 or higher applied every morning and reapplied every 2–3 hours outdoors.

THE FULL PICTURE
1

🥗 Eat seasonal, hydrating foods

Start with the 5 foods in this guide. Add coconut water, curd, and fresh vegetables to your daily meals. Reduce processed food and refined sugar — both accelerate summer skin damage.

Inside out
2

🧴 Cleanse gently, twice daily

Summer sweat and pollution clog pores faster. Use a gentle, pH-balanced cleanser morning and night — avoid harsh foaming cleansers that strip your skin and cause rebound oiliness.

Skin barrier first
3

☀️ Apply SPF every single morning

Non-negotiable in Indian summers. Broad-spectrum SPF 30 or higher, as the last step of your morning routine. Reapply every 2–3 hours if you're outdoors.

SPF is not optional
4

💧 Moisturise even in summer

Many people skip moisturiser in summer thinking their skin is already oily. A lightweight, water-based moisturiser actually helps regulate oil production and keeps the barrier intact.

Hydration balance
