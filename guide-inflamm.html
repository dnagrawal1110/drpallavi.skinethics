WHAT IS INFLAMMAGING?

Inflammation + Ageing = Inflammaging

Inflammaging is a state of chronic, low-grade, sterile inflammation — no infection, no visible wound — that persists for years in the dermis and silently accelerates biological ageing. First described by immunologist Claudio Franceschi, it explains why people age faster than their genetics alone would predict.

Unlike acute inflammation (which heals a cut in days and then stops), inflammaging is a background process running continuously. Think of it as an underground fire — nothing visible on the surface, but the heat is there 24 hours a day, slowly melting the framework that keeps skin tight and bouncy.

"No redness. No itching. No pain. But in your dermis — where collagen and elastin live — inflammatory enzymes called MMPs are being released continuously, breaking down the proteins that keep skin firm and youthful."

MMPs (Matrix Metalloproteinases) are your skin's demolition crew. In healthy ageing, they're balanced by repair signals. In inflammaging, they're chronically overactive — tearing down collagen faster than it can ever be rebuilt by any serum.

THE FOUR FUELS OF THE SILENT FIRE
🍬

Excess Sugar & Refined Carbs

High blood glucose triggers two parallel attacks: glycation (sugar stiffening collagen directly) and pro-inflammatory cytokine release (IL-1β, IL-6, TNF-α). Every spike in blood sugar is a small inflammatory event — and most people spike 3–5 times daily.

🏙️

Urban Pollution (PM2.5)

Fine particulate matter under 2.5 microns can penetrate the stratum corneum and reach the living dermis. Once inside, these particles trigger a continuous innate immune response — the body treating them as permanent, unresolvable invaders. Pune and Mumbai air quality directly drives inflammaging.

🧠

Chronic Mental Stress

Cortisol activates NF-κB — the master genetic switch for inflammation in skin cells. Chronic stress means chronically elevated NF-κB activity. Your stress is literally rewriting your skin's inflammatory programming on a daily basis.

☀️

Daily UV (Even Without Sunburn)

Sub-erythema UV — the daily dose below the sunburn threshold — generates reactive oxygen species (ROS) in the dermis continuously. Over years, this accumulated oxidative stress is a major and underestimated inflammaging driver.

THE COLLAGEN BREAKDOWN PATHWAY

Step by step: from trigger to visible ageing

1

Trigger activates dermal immune cells

Sugar spike, pollution particle, cortisol surge, or UV photon reaches the dermis and activates resident immune cells.

2

Pro-inflammatory cytokines released

IL-1β, IL-6, and TNF-α flood the dermal environment — the chemical language of inflammation.

3

MMP enzymes switched on

Cytokines activate Matrix Metalloproteinases — enzymes that specifically break down collagen and elastin fibres into fragments.

4

Collagen fibres cleaved

MMP-1 and MMP-3 cut Type I and III collagen into fragments. These fragments cannot provide structural support — skin begins to lose firmness.

5

Visible signs appear on surface

Fine lines, loss of bounce, skin laxity, dullness — this is inflammaging made visible. By this point, the process has been running for months or years.

PUTTING OUT THE FIRE — 5 APPROACHES
1

Anti-Inflammatory Topical Actives

Niacinamide (5%+) reduces MMP activation and strengthens the barrier against pollution. Centella asiatica directly inhibits inflammatory cytokines. Azelaic acid reduces inflammatory signalling. Green tea EGCG neutralises ROS at the surface. Use these consistently in your PM routine.

2

Antioxidant Serum — Every Single Morning

Vitamin C (L-ascorbic acid 10–20%) neutralises free radicals before they can trigger the inflammatory cascade. Vitamin E and ferulic acid extend Vitamin C's effectiveness significantly. This is your morning shield against pollution and UV-driven inflammaging — apply before SPF.

3

Dietary Intervention (The Highest-Impact Change)

Reduce glycaemic load: cut refined sugar, white flour, sugary drinks. Increase omega-3 fatty acids (fatty fish, flaxseed, walnuts) — EPA and DHA directly compete with the pro-inflammatory arachidonic acid pathway. Dietary changes show skin results in 8–12 weeks.

4

Stress Management — A Clinical Requirement

Cortisol management is a dermatological treatment, not optional lifestyle advice. Even 10 minutes of daily breathwork or meditation measurably reduces cortisol. Chronic elevated cortisol cannot be treated by any topical product — it must be addressed at the source.

5

SPF Every Day Without Exception

Mineral SPF (zinc oxide / titanium dioxide) blocks both UV and HEV light, preventing the daily sub-erythema UV dose that continuously fuels inflammaging. No single skincare habit has a higher return on investment for long-term collagen preservation.
