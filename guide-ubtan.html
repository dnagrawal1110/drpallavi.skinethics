BODY SKIN ≠ FACIAL SKIN
🦵

Body Skin

  • Thicker, tougher
  • Fewer oil glands
  • Less sun-exposed
  • Handles physical scrubbing well
  • Besan ubtan: ✅ Generally safe
🧑

Facial Skin

  • Thinner, more delicate
  • Higher oil gland density
  • Maximum sun exposure
  • Fragile protective barrier
  • Besan ubtan: ⚠️ Use with caution

The skin barrier — your face's security guard 🛡️

Think of it as a thin layer of oils and proteins that keeps moisture in and bacteria out. When healthy, skin looks glowing. When damaged — by harsh rubbing or strong ingredients — skin becomes red, dry, itchy, and breakout-prone.

Facial skin's barrier is much more fragile than body skin. It takes longer to recover, and damage accumulates over time.

WHAT ACTUALLY HAPPENS WHEN YOU APPLY BESAN ON YOUR FACE
🔬

Micro-tears in skin

Besan particles are jagged at a microscopic level. Rubbing them on the face creates tiny scratches that damage the skin barrier and trigger low-grade inflammation — often invisible to the eye.

⚗️

pH Disruption

Your skin's natural pH is slightly acidic (4.5–5.5). Besan is alkaline. Repeated use disrupts this balance, causing dryness, increased sensitivity, and — ironically — more acne.

💧

The "Squeaky Clean" Trap

That tight, dry feeling after ubtan isn't "cleanliness" — it's your skin signalling moisture loss. Besan has absorbed the natural oils your skin needed for protection.

WHO SHOULD AVOID IT ON THE FACE

Skip besan on your face entirely if you have:

🔴

Acne-Prone Skin

Rubbing on active pimples spreads bacteria and increases inflammation.

🌸

Sensitive Skin

Immediate redness, burning, or stinging is your barrier signalling damage.

🏜️

Dry / Dehydrated

Besan is naturally drying — it absorbs the very little moisture your skin already has.

🌅

Post-Sun Exposure

Never scrub already-stressed or sunburnt skin — it worsens damage significantly.

When is it safe? 🌿

On your elbows, knees, back, and body — absolutely. The skin there is thicker and handles physical exfoliation well. Besan + turmeric + curd is a brilliant body ubtan. The problem is specifically the face.

SAFER ALTERNATIVES FOR YOUR FACE
🍍

Enzyme Cleansers

Fruit extracts (papaya, pineapple) gently dissolve dead skin without scrubbing. No micro-tears, no friction.

All skin types
🥛

Gentle chemical exfoliant that hydrates while it exfoliates. Ideal for Indian skin — improves glow without irritation.

Best for Indian skin
🍯

Honey + Curd Mask

If you love DIY, this is the safer swap. Both are hydrating and don't disrupt pH or cause physical abrasion.

Safe DIY option
🌿

Gentle Gel Cleansers

A pH-balanced face wash already does what ubtan is supposed to do — cleanse and mildly exfoliate — without the damage.

Daily use
EXFOLIATION — OLD WAY vs. MODERN WAY
Physical

Besan, scrubs, grains

Manually scrubs off dead skin. High risk of micro-tears, irritation, and barrier damage. More isn't better.

Chemical (AHA/BHA)

Lactic, Glycolic, Salicylic Acid

Dissolves the "glue" holding dead skin cells together. Controlled, no abrasion, even glow without the scratches.

The bottom line 💡

Physical exfoliation isn't bad — it's just less precise and less controllable for facial skin. Chemical exfoliation gives you the same result more safely, especially if your skin is sensitive or acne-prone.

COMMON DIY MISTAKES TO AVOID
✕
Adding lemon juice to face packs: Lemon is highly acidic and can cause chemical burns or extreme sun sensitivity on Indian skin. Never apply directly to the face.
✕
Scrubbing harder thinking it's "cleaner": This is the most damaging myth in skincare. Harder scrubbing = more micro-tears = more pigmentation and premature ageing.
✕
Letting the mask dry completely: When a mask "cracks" on your face, it's pulling moisture out of your skin. Always wash off while still slightly damp.
✕
Skipping moisturiser after any DIY treatment: Every form of exfoliation temporarily compromises the barrier. Always follow with a moisturiser.
✕
No sunscreen after exfoliation: Any exfoliation — physical or chemical — makes skin temporarily more UV-sensitive. Sunscreen is non-negotiable the next morning.
🌟 SAFE SKINCARE CHECKLIST

Before trying any DIY remedy on your face:

✅
Patch test any DIY remedy on your neck or inner arm for 24 hours first
✅
Use curd or honey as a base — hydrating, pH-friendly, and gentle
✅
Always follow every treatment with a good moisturiser
✅
Limit facial exfoliation to once a week maximum
✅
Apply sunscreen the morning after any exfoliation
🚫
Never use lemon juice, raw sugar, or kitchen salt directly on your face
🚫
Never try DIY on sunburnt, irritated, or active-breakout skin
SEE A DERMATOLOGIST IF
⚠️ Consult Now

Don't delay if you notice:

  • Skin feels raw or stings when you apply moisturiser
  • Dark patches (post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation) after a DIY remedy
  • Acne worsening after home treatments
  • Persistent redness, peeling, or burning that won't settle
