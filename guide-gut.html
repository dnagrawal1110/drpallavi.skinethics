THE DEEPER TRUTH

Psoriasis, eczema, and alopecia areata are widely treated as skin diseases. But the real origin point for most cases is deeper: the gut and the immune system.

80% of your immune system lives in your gut. When the gut is inflamed, the immune system goes into overdrive — and the overflow shows on your skin.

LEAKY GUT — THE MECHANISM

When the gut lining weakens (Leaky Gut), tiny food particles and toxins slip into the bloodstream. Your immune system identifies them as invaders and attacks.

This overactive immune response is what drives:

• Psoriasis: immune system pushes skin cells to the surface 10× too fast → scaling, plaques
• Eczema: gut inflammation leaks through as skin barrier breakdown → redness, itching, rashes

WHAT TRIGGERS THE GUT INFLAMMATION

Dairy and gluten sensitivity

Common trigger foods that increase gut inflammation in predisposed individuals.

Refined sugar

Feeds bad gut bacteria and promotes inflammatory cytokine release.

Chronic stress

Stress hormone cortisol directly damages gut lining integrity.

Poor sleep

Sleep deprivation impairs gut barrier repair and amplifies immune over-reactivity.

THE ROOT CAUSE APPROACH
1

Identify and remove trigger foods

An elimination diet (removing dairy, gluten, and sugar for 4-6 weeks) can dramatically reduce flares in gut-driven skin conditions.

2

Heal the gut lining

Zinc carnosine, L-glutamine, and probiotics support gut barrier repair and reduce permeability.

3

Anti-inflammatory diet

Omega-3 rich foods, fermented foods (curd, kimchi), and high-fibre vegetables support healthy gut microbiome.

4

Medical management alongside

Topical treatments manage symptoms while root cause work happens underneath. SkinEthics takes a combined approach.

STEROIDS — THE INCOMPLETE ANSWER

Steroids and topical treatments put out the fire at the surface. But if gut inflammation continues, the fire reignites — often worse. True healing requires addressing the gut root cause, not just managing the skin symptoms.
