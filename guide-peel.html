Let me give you an honest answer. 🧴

Chemical peel सुनते ही डर लग जाता है — but that fear is based on misinformation, not reality. Most patients feel nothing more than a mild tingling that settles in a few minutes.

A chemical peel is a controlled skin renewal treatment. A special solution is applied that increases skin turnover, gently removes dead skin cells, and helps fresh, healthy skin surface. When done by a qualified dermatologist, it is precise, safe, and effective.

If you've been struggling with acne marks, pigmentation, dull skin, or uneven texture — this guide is for you.

"People don't avoid chemical peels because of pain. They avoid them because they don't understand how precisely this treatment is designed."
— Dr. Pallavi Ahire-Shelke
TYPES OF PEELS — SIMPLIFIED
I
Superficial · Mild

Fruit Acid Peels

Uses glycolic acid or lactic acid (AHAs). Great for a quick glow, mild detan, and improving skin texture. Almost no visible peeling — skin may just look brighter.

Best for: Dull skin, mild pigmentation, first-time peel patients

II
Medium Depth

TCA & Combination Peels

Goes deeper to target acne marks and significant pigmentation. You may see some actual skin shedding for a few days — like fine, flour-like peeling. Not dramatic, but visible.

Best for: Post-acne marks, melasma, open pores, uneven texture

III
Deep · Clinical

Phenol & Deep Peels

Targets severe scarring or deep wrinkles. Requires significant downtime and careful monitoring. Less common for routine skincare — always done in a clinical setting.

Best for: Severe scarring, deep wrinkles — always under dermatologist care

🔴Post-acne marks — Those stubborn brown or red spots left after pimples

☀️Sun tan & damage — Accumulated UV exposure that's affected your skin tone
MYTHS I BUST EVERY DAY IN CLINIC
✕"Chemical peels thin my skin"
✔Peels actually stimulate collagen production, which can make your skin feel firmer and healthier over time. Done correctly, they improve skin structure — they don't damage it.
✕"It's very painful"
✔Most people feel a slight tingling or mild warmth for 5–10 minutes during the procedure. It is entirely manageable and settles quickly. Pain is not a feature of a well-administered peel.
✕"My skin will peel off like a snake"
✔With modern mild-to-medium peels, shedding is often microscopic or appears as very mild dryness. You won't need to hide at home. The dramatic peeling images online are from older or deeper protocols.
✕"Peels will darken Indian skin"
✔When done correctly by a qualified dermatologist, peels are safe for all Indian skin tones. The risk of post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation exists only with improper protocols, incorrect acid choice, or unqualified procedures.
"The key is not whether to do a peel — it's which peel is right for your skin condition and tone."

The right peel, chosen correctly, can significantly improve how your skin looks and behaves over time. The formulation and depth must match your specific concern and skin type.

WHAT HAPPENS DURING THE PROCEDURE
1

🧼 Cleansing

The doctor cleans your face to remove all traces of oil, sunscreen, and makeup before any solution is applied.

2

🖌️ Application

The peel solution is applied with a brush or cotton tip in a precise, controlled manner — targeting specific concern areas.

3

💬 The Sensation

You'll feel a prickly, itchy, or warm sensation — this is normal and lasts only a few minutes. Most patients describe it as very manageable.

4

💧 Neutralisation

After the correct contact time, the doctor stops the peel with a neutralising solution or water. Timing is everything — this is why qualified supervision matters.

5

🛡️ Protection

A soothing cream and sunscreen are applied. You leave with protected, treated skin ready to begin its renewal process.

RECOVERY — DAY BY DAY

Day 1

Skin might look slightly pink or feel tight — similar to a very mild sunburn. Completely normal.

Day 2–3

Skin may look slightly darker or feel dry. This is the treated skin beginning its natural renewal process.

Day 4–7

Very fine, flour-like peeling may occur. Do not scrub it off — let it shed naturally. This is the old skin leaving.

Day 7+

Fresher, brighter, more even skin emerges. The visible glow typically appears around day 7–10 after the procedure.

3 Sessions+

Noticeable and lasting improvement in pigmentation, marks, and texture with a consistent series of sessions.

PRE-CARE & POST-CARE
Before the Peel
·Stop retinoids or strong actives 3 days before
·Avoid threading, waxing, or bleaching for a week
·Inform your doctor about all current medications
·Apply SPF daily in the week leading up to the peel
After the Peel
✔Sunscreen SPF 30+ every 3–4 hours — your new skin is sensitive
✔Moisturise generously — keep skin hydrated for healing
✔Use only gentle cleanser and moisturiser for the first 5 days
✔Never pick or pull at any peeling skin — causes permanent marks
HOW MANY SESSIONS?

Results are rarely one-and-done.

4–6

Sessions are typically needed for correcting pigmentation or acne marks.

Spaced 3–4 weeks apart — giving skin time to complete its renewal cycle between treatments.

You'll see a glow after one session. Lasting, deep correction takes a full course.

WHO SHOULD AVOID CHEMICAL PEELS
·Pregnant or breastfeeding women (some acids are off-limits — always consult your doctor)
·People with active skin infections, cold sores, or open wounds on the treatment area
·Those on certain oral acne medications (Isotretinoin) in the last 6 months
·Skin that is actively sunburned or inflamed on the day of the procedure

Important: Always get a chemical peel done by a qualified dermatologist. "At-home" high-strength peels are risky and can lead to chemical burns or worsened pigmentation — especially on Indian skin tones which are prone to post-inflammatory scarring.
