YOUR QUESTIONS — ANSWERED

Q: क्या दर्द होता है?

Think of a rubber band snap on your skin — that's the closest description. For sensitive areas, numbing cream is applied beforehand, which makes the procedure very manageable. Most patients are surprised by how comfortable it actually is.

Q: कितने sessions लगते हैं?

Typically 6 to 10 sessions for a full course. Sessions are spaced 4–6 weeks apart — this gap is important because not all hair follicles are active at the same time (hair grows in cycles). Rushing sessions means missing active follicles.

Q: क्या यह permanent है?

Not 100% permanent — but long-term reduction, yes. Most people achieve 80–90% hair reduction. A maintenance session once a year is enough to keep it that way. Compare that to waxing every 3–4 weeks for life.

Q: क्या यह Indian skin पर safe है?

Yes — when the right laser and trained hands are used. Nd:YAG, Diode, and Alexandrite lasers are considered safe for Indian skin tones. The key is correct settings calibrated to your Fitzpatrick skin type. This is why choosing a qualified dermatologist matters.

Q: Cost क्या है?

Per session cost varies by body area. But look at it long-term: laser is significantly more economical than waxing over 5–10 years — plus no more in-grown hairs, no waiting for hair to grow back, and no monthly appointments.

LASER TYPES FOR INDIAN SKIN
Nd:YAG

Best for Darker Indian Skin Tones

Deeper penetration, lower risk of pigmentation changes. The gold standard for darker Fitzpatrick types common in India.

Diode

Works Well Across Medium Skin Tones

Effective and widely used. A versatile option for medium skin tones with a strong safety profile when calibrated correctly.

Alexandrite

Suitable for Lighter Skin Tones

Fast treatment, effective for large areas. Best suited to lighter skin tones with finer or lighter hair.

WHEN TO AVOID LASER
⚠During pregnancy — avoid completely
⚠Active skin infection in the area — delay until resolved
⚠Recent sun exposure / active tan — wait 2 weeks
⚠Certain medications (e.g. photosensitising drugs) — disclose during consultation
