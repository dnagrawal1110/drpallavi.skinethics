Let me tell you what oily skin actually means 🔬

Oily skin means your sebaceous glands are overactive — they produce more sebum than your skin needs. But sebum is not the same as hydration. Sebum is an oil. Water is water. Your skin can be simultaneously oily on the surface and severely dehydrated at a cellular level — and this combination is far more common than most people realise.

When you strip your skin of moisture — through skipping moisturiser, over-cleansing, or using harsh actives without support — your skin's response is to produce even more oil to compensate. The very thing you were trying to reduce, you are making worse.

"In over a decade of practice, I have yet to meet an oily skin patient who needed less hydration. They all needed better hydration."
— Dr. Pallavi Ahire-Shelke
THE SCIENCE BEHIND IT
01

🧫 Sebum ≠ Hydration

Sebum is a lipid secretion. It protects the skin surface. It does not hydrate skin cells. These are two entirely different biological functions happening in different layers of the skin.

02

💧 Dehydrated Oily Skin Is Real

Oily skin that is also dehydrated is extremely common in India — triggered by hard water, humidity, air conditioning, and harsh cleansing habits.

03

🔄 The Compensation Cycle

When skin loses water, sebaceous glands produce more oil to protect the barrier. More oil means clogged pores. More clogged pores mean breakouts. Moisturiser breaks this cycle.

"Moisturiser does not cause acne. The wrong moisturiser might."

This is not a nuance — it is a clinical fact. The formulation is everything. Oily skin needs moisture delivered in the right vehicle: lightweight, water-based, and non-comedogenic.

HOW WE CHOOSE A MOISTURISER FOR OILY SKIN

When a patient with oily or acne-prone skin comes to me, here is exactly what I look for on the label — and what I tell them to avoid.

✔ Look For
✔ Gel or gel-cream texture

Delivers hydration without heaviness. Absorbs quickly, leaves no greasy residue.

✔ Non-comedogenic label

Tested to not block pores. The single most important label claim for oily skin.

✔ Hyaluronic Acid

Draws water into skin cells. Lightweight, oil-free, highly effective.

✔ Niacinamide

Regulates sebum production while strengthening the barrier.

✔ Centella Asiatica

Calms inflammation, supports barrier repair without heaviness.

✕ Avoid
✕ Heavy creams & butters

Shea, cocoa butter sit on oily skin and block pores. Formulated for dry skin.

✕ Fragrance & essential oils

Frequently comedogenic and inflammatory. Often hidden as "parfum".

✕ Alcohol-heavy formulas

Dries the surface while leaving deeper layers dehydrated.

✕ Petroleum-based occlusives

Vaseline and mineral oil are excellent for dry skin. For oily skin, they suffocate pores.

THE RIGHT WAY TO APPLY
1

🧴 Cleanse first — gently

Use a gentle, pH-balanced gel cleanser. Avoid antibacterial soaps or cleansers that leave skin feeling "squeaky clean" — that feeling means your barrier is stripped.

pH-balanced cleanse
2

💧 Apply on damp skin

Apply your moisturiser within 60 seconds of washing your face, while skin is still slightly damp. This locks in water rather than just sitting on top of a dry surface.

Damp skin application
3

✌️ Use a pea-sized amount

Oily skin needs a thin, even layer — not a thick application. A pea-sized amount spread across the entire face is sufficient. More product does not mean more hydration.

Less is more
4

☀️ Always follow with SPF in the morning

SPF is not optional for oily skin — UV exposure worsens sebum production and post-acne pigmentation. Choose a lightweight, non-comedogenic SPF 30 or higher.

SPF is step two

MYTHS I HEAR EVERY DAY IN CLINIC
✕"Moisturiser will make my skin more oily"
✔The opposite is true. When skin is properly hydrated, sebaceous glands receive the signal to slow down oil production. Consistent moisturisation reduces oiliness over time — within weeks.
✕"I have oily skin so my skin doesn't need extra hydration"
✔Oiliness is a surface phenomenon. Hydration happens at a cellular level. You can have an oily surface and dehydrated cells simultaneously — and most untreated oily skin patients in my clinic do.
✕"If I break out after a moisturiser, moisturisers cause my acne"
✔A breakout after a specific moisturiser means that moisturiser was not formulated correctly for oily skin. The answer is to change the formulation, not abandon moisturisation entirely.
